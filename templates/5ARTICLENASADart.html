An Honest Review:

NASA could unintentionally create a man made meteor shower

June 4th by Paul McBurney Jr

The NASA Dart program was founded to locate and destroy asteroids that could threaten the existance of humanity.

After years of development, the "asteroid redirection system" also known as DART is planned to be used to reflect asteroids away from earth.

The DART Mission will test whether not it is possible to redirect an asteroid away from earth.

However, the debris it would create could cause a medium sized meteor shower that can be seen from earth.

How Does DART Work?

NASA's DART mission aims to alter an asteroids course by using a spacecraft with a high velocity to crash into the space rock.

Even a minor alteration to the asteroids course can mean the difference between collision and no collision.


Back to Meteor Shower

According to studies done by NASA scientists, it is possible that a test DART collision with an asteroid could eject pieces of tiny space-rock.

The majority of rock ejected by the spacecraft will go unnoticed. However, a select few might near earth.

Fortunatley, we won't need to worry because most of the ejected rock that reaches earth will burn up in the atmosphere.

Also, because of gravity, most of the space debris will be pulled back into the asteroid.

It'll be cool to see that space debris light up the pitch black sky!


Conclusion

Even though most of us will go unaffected, it's still pretty interesting to know that we might be seeing some man made meteor showers soon.

Hopefully NASA can perfect their DART program to the point where we won't have to worry about any meteor showers or potential asteroid collisions.

It's good to know that NASA is developing asteroid defense systems before it's too late.

Overall, it would be a shame if a system meant to keep asteroids away from us, unintentionally causes a meter shower.
